## Semester-III

## BTT301 YARN MANUFACTURING-I

## UNIT I

Cotton Ginning:- Introduction of ginning process, Functions of ginning machines, Types of Ginning machines, Pre and post ginning machines and their objects, Factors affecting ginning performance, Influence  of  ginning  on  fibre,  yarn  and  fabric  quality,  Pressing  and  bailing  of  Indian  and  foreign cotton.  Objects  of  mixing,  different  types  of  mixing  &amp;  blending.  Difference  between  mixing  &amp; blending. Process flow chart for carded &amp; combed yarn manufacturing.

## UNIT II

Objects of Blow room for natural and synthetic fibres, Principles of opening, cleaning and mixing. Principles  of  various  opening,  cleaning  and  mixing  machines  of  blow  room  line,  auxiliary  devices used  in  blow  room  ,.  Various  zones  and  machines  used  in  each  zone  of  blow  room  line  , Conventional blow room and  Lap formation mechanism.

## UNIT III

Automatic  bale opener, Mild openers/course cleaning machines  such as Maxi-flow/ Uniclean/Vario-clean, modern mixing / Blending machines , Fine cleaning machines such as  cleanomat, flexiclean, Intensive cleaners , Waste extracted at various openers and beaters, Cleaning efficiency of  different  machines,  Neps  generation  in  blow  room.  calculations  of  speed,  production,  and pertaining to waste and cleaning efficiency  in blow room.  Latest developments in blow room.

## UNIT IV

Lap  feed  and  chute  feed  system  ;  Principle  and  concept  of  chute  feed  to  card  ,Advantages  and limitations of chute feed system, Objects of carding, functioning of various parts of  Carding , Theory of carding - Opening of fibre mass - Carding action , Web formation and coiling mechanism , Fibre configuration in card sliver, Leveling action, control of Fibre breakage and neps in carding , waste extraction and cleaning efficiency ,

## UNIT V

Stripping and grinding, Stripping action and carding action, Card Clothing, evolution and Metallic wire details -- Card wire mounting, Tandem carding, Auto leveller used in carding, Various setting in carding machine and implications, Calculation on draft and production of carding. Latest developments in carding.

## BTT302

## UNIT I

Objects of winding process, classification of winding, (manual &amp; automatic), various latest winding machines with detailed construction and working, Description of various winding accessories.

## UNIT II

Geometrical  aspects:  -  Cone  angle,  angle  of  wind,  wind  per  double  traverse,  surface  speed, traverse  speed,  winding  speed,  calculation of  winding  speed,  production/spindle   &amp; per machine, and efficiency.

## UNIT III

Objectives  of  pirn  winding,  its  advantage  over  rewound  weft,  Details  of  semi-automatic  and automatic pirn winding machines w. r. t drive to spindles, traverse, tensioning yarn path. Pirn build: length  of  wind,  chase  length,  diameter,  bunch,  tail  ends  etc.  their  importance  during  weaving process.  Calculations:  -  Average  pirn  diameter,  winding  speed,  production  /  spindle  / &amp;  per machine, efficiency, number of looms fed by spindle.

## UNIT IV

Objectives  of  warping,  precautions  to  be  considered  in  the  process,  classification  of  warping process- (beam warping, sectional warping, ball warping), Latest Warping machine: - construction and working, Creel: -framing (requirements, length, height, pitch, etc.) pegs, tensioning arrangements guides, blow fan,  types  of  creels  (parallel,  V, V-nose etc.), Principles of operation of beam warping and sectional warping. Sectional warping machines, Waxing attachment, computerized warping machines. Calculations related to warping

## UNIT V

Objectives of sizing and sizing terminology, achieving the objectives through sizing paste constituents, concepts of sizing process: hank sizing, ball warp sizing, Slasher sizing, multi- cylinder sizing,  description  of  sizing  ingredients,  Latest  developments  in  sizing  process  by  various  sizing machine  manufacturers.  Drawing-in:  Objectives,  process  description,  reed  count  system,  manual drawing-in,  semi-  automatic  drawing-in  process,  Knotting  process  and  its  limitations.  Calculations related to sizing and drawing-in
