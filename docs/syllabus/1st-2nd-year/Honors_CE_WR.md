## Syllabus

## HTWR401        ADVANCED FLUID MECHANICS

## Course Outcomes: This course will enable students to

CO1. Apply law of conservation of energy and mass during fluid flow.

- CO2. Analyze the problems of potential fluid flow.
- CO3. Apply equation of fluid flow for solving the problems.
- CO4. Apply laminar boundary layer equation for addressing the flow.
- CO5. Solve the problems of unsteady flow in pipes.

| Unit-1   | Equations governing fluid flow: Reynolds transport theorem, law of conservation of mass-continuity equation, law of conservation of momentum- equation of motion, law of conservation of energy- energy equation.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |   8 |
|----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Potential fluid flow: Standard flow pattern- uniform flow, source, irrotational vortex circulation, doublet, source and sink, vortex pair; source and vortex-spiral flow; source and uniform flow-flow past a half body; doublet and uniform flow-flow past a half body; source, sink and uniform flow- flow past a Rankine body; doublet and uniform flow-flow past cylinder, Doublet, vortex and uniform flow-flow past a cylinder with circulation; Magnus effect.                                                                                      |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Viscous flow and flow instability: Equation of motion-Navier-Stokes equation, Exact and approximate solutions of N-S equation, creeping motion, theory of instability of laminar flow- methods of small disturbance, stability analysis, Orr- Sommerfeld equation, solution of OSE equation- neutral stability curve, stages of transition from laminar to turbulent flow, factors affecting transition from laminar to turbulent flow.                                                                                                                    |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Boundary layer theory: Factors affecting growth of boundary layer, momentum thickness, displacement thickness, energy thickness, order of magnitude analysis, Prandtl's boundary layer equation, exact solution of laminar boundary layer equation for flow on a flat plate, von Karman momentum integral equation and its application in computation of boundary shear stress, drag, local and average coefficients of friction for laminar and turbulent boundary layers, factors affecting separation of boundary layer and its control.                |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Unsteady flow in pipe: Smooth and rough turbulent flow and their velocity distributions, Moody's diagram - friction factor and its variation with Reynolds number and relative roughness. Introduction to Pressure transients, cause of transients, Types of transient flow, true pressure transient incompressible flow, establishment of flow, elastic theory, rigid pipeline, elastic pipeline, time series event following sudden closure,Water hammer, Rigid and elastic water column theories, methods of analysis, surge protection and surge tank. |   8 |

## HTWR501                                          FLUVIAL HYDRAULICS

## Course Outcomes: This course will enable students to

- CO1. To understand and solve problems of sediments.
- CO2. Analyze thebed load and suspended load transport problems for uniform and non uniform bed material.
- CO3. Design stable channel and understand sediment sampling and control.
- CO4. Understand and analyze Bed level variations, local scour, degradation and aggradation.
- CO5. Analyze  physical  and  mathematical  models  and  design  guide  bunds  and  other  river training banks.

| Unit-1   | The sediment problems, properties of sediments, incipient motion of uniform and non-uniform sediments. Bed forms and channel resistance.   |   8 |
|----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Bed load and suspended load transport for uniform and non-uniform bed material, total load equations.                                      |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Sediment sampling, Stable channel design and sediment control.                                                                             |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Bed level variations, local scour, degradation, aggradation and reservoir sedimentation.                                                   |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Physical and mathematical models. Design of guide bunds and other river training banks.                                                    |   8 |

## HTWR601                                      ADVANCED HYDROLOGY

## Course Outcomes: This course will enable students to

- CO1. Analyze Hydrologic system and budget, Hydrologic Inputs.
- CO2. Analyze Hydrologic Abstractions; infiltration, depression, storage evapotranspirations.
- CO3. Analyze stream flow.
- CO4. Analyze Mathematical Linear and Nonlinear models.
- CO5. Analyze Hydrological Routing.

| Unit-1   | Introduction: Hydrologic system and hydrologic budget, fundamental laws of hydrology; atmospheric water vapor. Hydrologic inputs: Precipitation and its forms, snowfall and rainfall; measurement techniques and space-time characteristics.   |   8 |
|----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Hydrologic abstractions: Infiltration, depression, storage, evapotranspiration; measurement techniques and their modeling.                                                                                                                     |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Stream flow: Measurement techniques, space-time characteristics, rating curves System Approach: Unit Hydrograph, IUH, GIUH.                                                                                                                    |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Mathematical modeling : Linear and Nonlinear models, Physically based models.                                                                                                                                                                  |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Hydrological routing: Flood forecasting, Advance regression and correlation analysis. Advanced Method of Frequency Analysis: Outliers, Time series analysis.                                                                                   |   8 |

## HTWR701                                GROUND WATER ENGINEERING

## Course Outcomes: This course will enable students to

- CO1. Understand  role  of  ground  water  in  hydrological  cycle  and  analyze  flow  and  storage characteristics of aquifers.
- CO2. Analyze and apply Governing equations for ground water flow.
- CO3. Analyze Wells and Well hydraulics.
- CO4. Apply Ground water conservation.
- CO5. Analyze ground water flow modeling.

| Unit-1   | Introduction: Definition of groundwater, role of groundwater in hydrological cycle, groundwater bearing formations, classification of aquifers, flow and storage characteristics of aquifers, Darcy's law, anisotropy and heterogeneity.                                                          |   8 |
|----------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Governing equations for groundwater flow: Dupuit-Forchheimer assumptions, general differential equations governing groundwater flows, analytical solutions.                                                                                                                                       |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Wells and well hydraulics: Different types of wells, construction of wells, steady and unsteady state solutions for confined, unconfined and leaky aquifers, effect of boundaries, method of images, pumping test analysis.                                                                       |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Groundwater conservation: Regional groundwater budget; resource assessment; estimation of recharge, Indian practice, artificial recharge Groundwater Quality: General problem of contamination of groundwater, sources, remedial and preventive measures, seawater intrusion in coastal aquifers. |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Ground water flow modelling: Role of groundwater flow models, reference to hydraulic, Hele-Shaw and analog models, introduction to numerical modeling. Planning of Groundwater Development: constraints on the development, role of flow models, optimal groundwater development.                 |   8 |

## HTWR801               HYDRAULIC MACHINERY AND WATER POWER PLANTS

## Course Outcomes: This course will enable students to

- CO1. Understand role of hydropower in a power system and estimate water power potential.
- CO2. Analyze general arrangement of run of river plants.
- CO3. Analyze and design of Penstocks.
- CO4. Analyze and design Trash racks and intakes.
- CO5. Analyze and design turbines in power plants.

| Unit-1   | Water power: Introduction, sources of energy, role of hydropower in a power system. Estimation of Water Power Potential: Flow duration curves of gauge and ungauge streams, load curve, load factor, capacity factor, utilization factor, diversity factor, load duration curve, firm power, secondary power, prediction of load.   |   8 |
|----------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Types of hydro-power plants: Run of river plants, general arrangement of run of river plants, valley dam plants, diversion canal plants, high head diversion plants, storage and pondage, pumped storage power plants.                                                                                                              |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Penstocks: General classification, design criteria, economical diameter, losses, anchor blocks, valves, bends and manifolds.                                                                                                                                                                                                        |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Trash racks: Types, losses, design, stability. Intakes: Types, losses, air entrainment, anti-vortex device, air vent, power channels, fore bay tunnel.                                                                                                                                                                              |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Turbines: Introduction, types of turbines, hydraulics of turbines, velocity triangles, draft tubes, cavitation in turbines, turbine model testing, characteristics of turbines. Transients caused by turbine, load acceptance and rejection, resonance in penstocks.                                                                |   8 |
