## Syllabus

| HTPE401                | PETROLEUM EXPLORATION AND DRILLING   |
|------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| EXAM DURATION: 3 HOURS | SEMESTER: IV(EVEN)                   |
|                        | PREREQUISITE: NIL                    |

## OBJECTIVE:

- The syllabus for petroleum exploration should be aimed at the students to have a broad knowledge of exploration  history  in  India  and  Gain  an  understanding  of  the  fundamental  principles  and  processes involved in petroleum exploration, including the nature and physical properties of petroleum, through theoretical study and case analysis.
- Explore the origins and formation of petroleum, unconventional sources such as gas hydrates, and their significance in modern exploration practices, supplemented by insightful case studies.
- Delve into the mechanisms of migration and accumulation of hydrocarbons, reservoir formation, and geological structures crucial for petroleum entrapment, supported by real-world case studies.
- Acquire  knowledge  of  gravity  and  magnetic  methods  in  petroleum  exploration,  including  Newton's gravitational law, measurement techniques, and their practical applications through hands-on exercises and analysis.
- Develop  proficiency  in  various  exploration  methodologies  such  as  geological,  geochemical,  remote sensing, and seismic surveys, coupled with case studies to illustrate their real-world applications and effectiveness.

## COURSE OUTCOME:

On successful completion of the course, the student will be able to:

- have a broad-based understanding of seismic exploration, viz its acquisition methods, processing and interpretation.
- know these methods with the other subjects so that they can opt for upstream industry jobs.

| HTPE501                | PETROLEUM REFINING   |
|------------------------|----------------------|
| EXAM DURATION: 3 HOURS | SEMESTER: V(ODD)     |
|                        | PREREQUISITE: NIL    |

## OBJECTIVE:

- Develop a comprehensive understanding of the petroleum industry, including its structure, types of crude oil, and the composition and properties of various refinery feedstocks.
- Gain insight into the essential processes and units involved in refining crude oil, covering desalting, distillation, dehydration, fractionation, and separation of key petroleum products.
- Explore advanced refining techniques such as cracking (thermal, hydro, catalytic), coking processes (delayed, flexi, fluid), and calcinations, along with processes for sulfur removal and enhancement (reforming, isomerization, alkylation, polymerization, hydro desulfurization).
- Acquire knowledge of safety protocols and environmental regulations governing refining operations, while also exploring emerging technologies shaping the future of petroleum refining.
- Analyze future trends in the industry, including the shift towards renewable fuels, the development of the hydrogen economy, and the adoption of sustainable practices, to prepare for evolving challenges and opportunities.

## COURSEOUTCOME:

On successful completion of the course, the student will be able to:

- Gain  a  comprehensive  understanding  of  the  petroleum  industry,  and  develop  insights  into  refining processes, units, and key environmental and safety considerations.
- Proficiency in Crude Oil Distillation principles and acquire skills in distillation operations.
- Understand the role of each process in transforming crude oil into valuable products.
- Acquire skills to integrate treating processes for emission reduction.
- Explore  emerging  technologies  in  petroleum  refining  and  critically  evaluate  future  trends,  including renewable fuels, the hydrogen economy, and sustainable practices.

| HTPE601                | PETROLEUM PRODUCTS CHARACTERIZATION   |
|------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| EXAM DURATION: 3 HOURS | SEMESTER:VI (EVEN)                    |
|                        | PREREQUISITE: NIL                     |

## OBJECTIVE:

- Understand  the  composition  of  petroleum  and  its major  classes of products (fuels, lubricants, petrochemicals),  alongside  the  significance  of  characterization  in  the  industry  and  adherence  to regulatory standards.
- Explore key properties of petroleum products including vapor pressure, octane number, viscosity, and sulfur content, and master elemental analysis techniques essential for quality assessment.
- Familiarize with international standards such as ASTM and ISO for petroleum testing, and learn about various analytical techniques like IR, UV-Vis, GC, LC, NMR, and XRD used in quality control.
- Gain proficiency in measuring density, viscosity, surface tension, and other properties crucial for quality evaluation, along with testing methods for oxidation and thermal stability of lubricants and additives.
- Dive  into  advanced  analyses  of  petroleum  components  like  olefins,  aromatics,  and  alkanes,  and understand polymer characterization methods, wax properties, and quality control measures for specialty chemicals, utilizing cutting-edge petrochemical characterization techniques.

## COURSE OUTCOME:

On successful completion of the course, the student will be able to:

- Comprehensive understanding of petroleum composition.
- Understand the significance of aromatics, olefins quantification, and thermal stability testing.
- Gain in-depth knowledgequality control and characterization techniques.
- Expertise in lubricants and lubricant additives characterization.
- Proficient in characterizing petroleum products.

| HTPE701                | PETROLEUM DOWNSTREAM PROCESSING   |
|------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| EXAM DURATION: 3 HOURS | SEMESTER: VII (ODD)               |
|                        | PREREQUISITE: NIL                 |

## OBJECTIVE:

- Understand  the  operations  and  products  of  refineries,  distinguishing  between  downstream  and  upstream processes and recognizing the role of downstream engineers in fractionation and product manufacturing.
- Explore  finishing  processes  such  as  sweetening,  sulfur  conversion,  solvent  extraction,  and  hydrotreating, crucial for refining high-quality petroleum products through solvent deasphalting and dewaxing.
- Master techniques  for  enhancing  octane  and  cetane  ratings,  including  isomerization  and  reforming,  while also learning about aromatics production and corrosion control in processes like bitumen blowing.
- Gain proficiency in product blending and quality control, covering gasoline, diesel, jet fuel, lubricants, and specialty products, ensuring optimal performance and compliance with industry standards.
- Analyze  environmental  considerations  in  downstream  treatment,  including  waste  management,  recycling, and  regulatory  compliance,  while  also  exploring  future  trends  in  downstream  engineering  to  address evolving challenges sustainably.

## COURSEOUTCOME:

On successful completion of the course, the student will be able to:

- Understand the Downstream Petroleum Industry.
- Understand the Fundamentals of Refinery Operations.
- Explore Advanced Processes and Energy Management.
- Develop Skills in Product Blending and Quality Control.
- Gain Knowledge about Emerging Technologies and Sustainability.

| HTPE801                | PETROCHEMICAL TECHNOLOGY   |
|------------------------|----------------------------|
| EXAM DURATION: 3 HOURS | SEMESTER:VIII (EVEN)       |
|                        | PREREQUISITE: NIL          |

## OBJECTIVE:

- Gain insights into the current landscape and future trends of the global and Indian petrochemical industry, including the classification of petrochemicals and their significance in various applications.
- Understand the processes involved in petrochemical feedstock preparation, including cracking of ethane/propane and naphtha/gas oil, as well as syngas production, for the generation of key building blocks.
- Explore the technology behind the production of essential petrochemicals such as methanol, formaldehyde, ethylene oxide, glycol, and various aromatic compounds, crucial for a range of industrial applications.
- Examine the properties, applications, and production technologies of commodity polymers like polyethylene, polypropylene, and polystyrene, along with engineering polymers such as ABS plastic and nylon 6.
- Delve into the properties, applications, and production processes of thermoset polymers like epoxy resin and  unsaturated  polyester  resin,  as  well  as  rubber,  understanding  their  diverse  roles  in  industry  and manufacturing.

## COURSE OUTCOME:

On successful completion of the course, the student will be able to:

- Explore  the  global and  Indian  petrochemical  industries' current and  future trends. Classification, importance, and uses of petrochemicals
- Understand  petrochemical  feedstock  preparation,  petrochemical  feedstock  classification,  and  petroleum feedstock preparation.
- Comprehension of the Manufacturing Process of Various Commercially Significant Petrochemicals from C1 and C2
- Acquire an understanding of the  production  of  several  petrochemicals that  are  economically  significant from aromatics
- Obtain knowledge  regarding the properties, applications, and production technologies of diverse commodity polymers.
