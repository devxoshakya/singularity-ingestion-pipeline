## Detailed Syllabus

| BHT 301   | TEXTILE FIBERS   |
|-----------|------------------|

## Objective:

1. To  enable  the  students  to  learn  about  classification  and  molecular  structure  of  Textile Fiber.
2. To enable the students to know about the different cellulosic fibers and their properties.
3. To enable the students to know about the different protein fibers, their properties and applications.
4. To enable the students to know about different regenerated fibers, their properties and applications.
5. To enable the students to know about the production techniques of different Synthetic Fibers, their properties and applications.

| Unit   | Topics                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |   Lectures |
|--------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| I      | Introduction: Definitions of: Textile Fiber, staple, filament, coarser & finer fiber, monomer, polymer, and co-polymer. Classification of Textile Fibers; Essential & Desirable Properties of Textile Fibers; Advantages & Disadvantages of Natural and Manmade Fibers. Concepts of: Molecular Weight, Degree of Polymerization, Orientation and Crystallinity, Characteristics of Fiber Forming Polymer.                                                                                              |         12 |
| II     | Natural Cellulosic Fibers: Cotton - Introduction, Morphology, Polymer System, Physical and Chemical Properties, and its Applications. Jute- Retting and Fiber Extraction Process, Properties of Jute Fiber and its Applications, Flax- Extraction Process, Properties of Flex Fiber and Applications. Banana- Physical, chemical characteristics and its Applications. Mudar- Introduction, Properties and its Applications.                                                                           |         12 |
| III    | Protein fibers: Silk- Verities of Silk, Life Cycle of Silk worm, Structure of Silk Fiber and its Characteristics, Silk Polymer System, Chemical and Physical Properties of Silk and its Applications. Wool - Morphology, Polymer System, Complex Structure of Wool, Chemical and Physical Properties of wool and its Applications.                                                                                                                                                                     |         12 |
| IV     | Manmade Fiber Production Techniques: Principle of Manmade Fiber Production, Wet Spinning, Dry Spinning, Melt Spinning. Regenerated Fibers: Viscose - Manufacturing Process, Microscopic Appearance of Viscose, Polymer System, Physical, Thermal, Chemical properties and its Applications. Lyocell- Manufacturing Process, Physical, Thermal, Chemical Properties and its Applications. Cuprammonium & Polynosic- Manufacturing Process, Physical, Thermal, Chemical Properties and its Applications. |         12 |
| V      | Synthetic Fibers: Polyester, Nylon-6 & Nylon-66, Acrylic- Manufacturing Process, Polymer System, Fiber Morphology, Chemical, Physical, Thermal Properties and its Applications. High Performance Fibers- Introduction and Application of Aramid, Nomex, Kevlar and Teflon Fiber. Post Spinning Operations- Drawing, Heat Setting and Texturing. Recent Development in Polymeric High Performance Fibers.                                                                                               |         12 |

## Outcomes:

Upon completion of the course, the students will be able: -

1. To know about the classification and molecular structurer of Textile Fiber.
2. 4 2. To know about the concept of different cellulosic fibers and their properties.
3. To know about different protein fibers, their properties and applications.
4. To know about different regenerated fibers, their properties and applications.
5. To  know  about  the  production  techniques  of  different  Synthetic  Fibers,  their  properties  and applications.

| BHT 302   | HANDLOOM WEAVING TECHNOLOGY   |
|-----------|-------------------------------|

## Objective:

1. To enable the students to learn about the winding process and classification yarn faults.
2. To enable the students to know about the different types of yarn package and warping process.
3. To enable the students to know about the various parts of Handloom and their function.
4. To enable the students to know about working of Handloom and types of reed.
5. To enable the students to know about the yarn numbering systems.

| Unit   | Topics                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |   Lectures |
|--------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| I      | Objects of winding, principles of cheese and cone winding machines, drum and precision winding, uniform build of yarn package, control of balloons, Classification of yarn faults and its removal, concepts in yarn clearing -mechanical, optical and electronic clearers, knotters and splicers, weftwinding, study of modern automatic winders. Primitive Charka, High Speed Charka, Pirn and bobbin winding for Handlooms.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |         12 |
| II     | Yarn packages - hanks, cones, cheeses, and spools - Characteristics and use; essential characteristic of warp and weft, Objectives of warping, peg warping, vertical warpingand sectional warping, objectives and importance of sizing of cotton yarn, ingredients used in sizemixture for handloom industry and their functions, various forms of sizing- hank sizing and street warp sizing. Characteristics of ideal sizing, common defects during sizing- causes and remedies.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |         12 |
| III    | Evolution of handlooms, Various parts of a handloom and their functions, types of handlooms- Throw shuttle handloom, Fly shuttle pit loom and frame loom. Relationship between type of handloom and the product, passage of warp in frame loom, motions of a handloom, definitions of primary, secondary & auxiliary, motions. Types of shed formations- center closed shed, bottom closed shed, top closed shed, open shed and semi open shed - advantages, disadvantages and shedding devices, shedding mechanism of a handloom using treadles and Heald reversing motions - roller system, pulley reversing system and jack and lam rod system.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |         12 |
| IV     | Picking mechanism of a handloom, types of shuttles - throw shuttle, fly shuttle and roller shuttle, design and suitability; beat-up: Open shed beat-up, closed shed beat up and crossed shed beat-up in handlooms, different types of reed: Bamboo reed, steel reed and all metal steel reed, suitability for various fabrics; let off motion in handlooms - ratchet wheel and pawl, rope and weight, rope lever and weight motions, take up motion in handloom - poker rod, ratchet wheel & pawl, auxiliary motions of a handloom - temple motion and terry motion, handloom dobbies - lattice dobby, barrel dobby and vertical dobby. Read Count - dents per linear space and groups of dents per linear space models. Stockport System of Reed count - relation between reed count, number of ends per dent, ends per inch, cloth width, reed width and total number of ends. Heald count calculations. |         12 |
| V      | Introduction to numbering of yarns: indirect system of numbering of yarns - New English cotton, New French, metric, worsted, Woolen Yorkshire Skein, linen, spun yarn, Direct system of numberingof yarns - Denier metric, Tex; Conversion of count of yarn - indirect to indirect, direct to direct, indirect to direct, direct to indirect systems. Determination of Equivalent/Resultant count of folded yarns with and without contraction during twisting, Costing of folded yarn, Average count of warp.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |         12 |

## Outcomes:

Upon completion of the course, the students will be able: -

1. To know about the winding process and classification yarn faults.

3. To know about the various parts of Handloom and their function.
4. To know about working of Handloom and types of reed.

## BHT401

## Objective:

2. To enable the students to know about the Singeing, Desizing, Bleaching and Mercerization.
3. To enable the students to know about the preparatory process of protein fibers.
4. To enable the students to know about preparatory processing machineries.
5. To enable the students to know about the classification of textile auxiliaries.

| Unit   | Topics                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |   Lectures |
|--------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| I      | Role of water & its quality for wet processing, hard water and soft water. Basic concepts of surfactants in textile processing, Natural and added impurities in textiles, Preparatory process sequences for cotton, wool, silk, polyester, nylon, acrylic and blends.                                                                                                                   |         12 |
| II     | Singeing: Gas singeing, Desizing: Hydrolytic and Oxidative systems, Scouring: Solvent, Conventional and Enzymatic systems, Bleaching with Sodium hypochlorite, Hydrogen peroxide, Sodium chlorite. Optical brightening agents (OBA), mechanism and its application on all fibres. Mercerization of cotton, physical and chemical aspects of mercerization, yarn and fabric mercerizing. |         12 |
| III    | Preparatory processes for protein fibres, wool: Carbonization, scouring, bleaching. Degumming and bleaching of silk. Preparatory processes for synthetic fibres and blends: Objective of heat setting, types of heat setting, working of stenters. Scouring and bleaching of synthetic fibres and blends. Assessment of desizing, scouring, bleaching and mercerization.                |         12 |
| IV     | Brief introduction to preparatory processing machineries - Batch machines: Pressure Kiers, Vaporloc machines, Jigger, Winch, Semi continuous and continuous machines: J-Box, Padding mangles                                                                                                                                                                                            |         12 |
| V      | General Consideration and classification of textile auxiliaries, Preparation of detergents, comparison between soaps and detergents, Physical principles involved in detergency conditions for efficient detergency.                                                                                                                                                                    |         12 |

## Outcomes:
