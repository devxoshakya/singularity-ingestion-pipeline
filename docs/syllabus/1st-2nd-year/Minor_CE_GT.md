## Syllabus

## MNGT401           INTRODUCTION TO SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT

## Course Outcomes: After learning this course, students will be able to:

- CO1. To understand the fundamentals of sustainable development.
- CO2. To realize the connection between sustainability and socio-economic development.
- CO3. To analyze various dimensions of sustainable development.
- CO4. To understand the framework of sustainability.
- CO5. To develop understanding of sustainable development through case studies.

| Unit-1   | Introduction to sustainable development: Need & concept of sustainability; Objectives of Sustainable Development, Population, Poverty and Pollution, Environment and Development linkages, vision of sustainable development (MDGs & SDGs); Nexus between technology & sustainable development; Historical development and Current practices, international efforts in the direction of sustainable development and their outcome.   |   8 |
|----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Socio-economic development & sustainability: Fundamentals of ecology - types of ecosystems & interrelationships, factors influencing sustainability of ecosystems, needs of present and future generation; issues, requirements for sustainability: food security and agriculture, renewable resources - water and energy, non-renewable resources.                                                                                  |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Sustainable development: various dimensions: Components of sustainability - Complexity of growth and equity - Social, economic and environmental dimensions of sustainable development, current challenges - natural, political, socio-economic imbalance; needs of present and future generation Sustainability in national and regional context.                                                                                   |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Framework for Sustainability: Analytical frameworks in sustainability studies, sustainability metrics: criteria and indicators, Hurdles to Sustainability ,Operational guidelines, current metrics and limitations; metrics for mapping and measuring sustainable development, Science and Technology for sustainable development.                                                                                                   |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Case studies &projects on sustainable development: Village resources (broad perspectives); visit to rural area with special attention to current challenges and thematic areas; village social hierarchy &economy; application of renewable energy and R-R-R concept.                                                                                                                                                                |   8 |

## MNGT601          GREEN TECHNOLOGIES &amp; RENEWABLE ENERGY SYSTEMS

## Course Outcomes: After learning this course, students will be able to:

- CO1. To understand the significance of green technologies.
- CO2. To analyze cleaner development mechanism (CDM).
- CO3. To understand green production techniques and eco friendly processes.
- CO4. To understand various aspects of green buildings.
- CO5. To analyze renewable energy systems as a part of green technology.

| Unit-1   | Fundamentals of green technology: Twelve principles of green chemistry, definition & significance of Green technology, factors affecting green technology. Role of green technologies in sustainable development, industrial ecology, role of industrial ecology in green technology.                                                               |   8 |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Cleaner development mechanism: Cleaner development mechanisms, role of industry; reuse, reduce and recycle, raw material substitution; wealth from waste; carbon credits, carbon trading, carbon sequestration, eco labeling; Oxidation technology for waste water treatment- Cavitation, Fenton chemistry, photocatalysis and hybrid processes.    |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Eco friendly processes: Energy efficient design of processes- photo, electro and sono chemical methods, microwave assisted synthesis, Eco-friendly and cost effective materials, recyclable and environment friendly materials, green production systems.                                                                                           |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Green buildings & sustainable urbanization: Basic Features, advantages & limitations, Energy efficient buildings- methods for increasing energy efficiency of buildings, building components, ventilation system, Energy need and supply, use of solar photovoltaic system and sun-tracking system, Sustainable cities, Sustainable transportation. |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Renewable energy: Various renewable energy sources, Solar Energy Systems: Solar radiations data; Solar energy collection, Storage and applications, Hydro Energy Systems: Resource assessment of micro and small hydro power: Wind Energy Systems, bio-fuels, fuel cells.                                                                           |   8 |

## MNGT801                  SOLAR ENERGY: TECHNOLOGY AND APPLICATIONS

## Course Outcomes: After learning this course, students will be able to:

- CO1. To understand basic concepts of solar energy.
- CO2. To understand the fundamentals and applications of solar thermal energy.
- CO3. To understand various aspects of solar photo voltaic energy.
- CO4. To analyze various applications of solar energy.
- CO5. To understand the systems utilizing combination of different sources of energy.

| Unit-1   | Introduction to solar energy: overview of global and Indian energy scenario, need of solar energy propagation of solar radiation from the sun to earth; solar radiation spectrum, extra-terrestrial and terrestrial radiation, solar energy measuring instruments; solar radiation estimation.   |   8 |
|----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Fundamentals of solar thermal energy: fundamentals of thermal collectors; flat plate collectors, thermal energy storage systems; solar thermal and PV.                                                                                                                                           |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Fundamentals of solar PV: Principles and performance analysis, modules, array, standalone PV systems, advances in solar thermal collectors and PV technologies.                                                                                                                                  |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Solar energy systems & applications: Solar energy collection, Storage and utilization; Solar water & air heating; Power generation; Refrigeration and Air- conditioning; water pumping, water heating, water purification, solar fuels etc.                                                      |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Integrated energy systems: Concept of integration of conventional and non- conventional energy resources and systems; integrated energy system design and economics.                                                                                                                             |   8 |
