## HTGT401

## Syllabus ENGINEERING GEOLOGY

## Course Outcome: This course will enable students

- CO1. To describe weathering process and mass movements.
- CO2. To explain various properties of minerals and their engineering significance.
- CO3. To identify various types of rocks.
- CO4. To interpret the importance of different geological features and their effects and to apply the principles of geological investigations in civil engineering structures.
- CO5. To define a role of geotechnical engineer as rock specialist/mining engineer/tunnel specialist for proper execution of any infrastructure project.

| Unit-1   | Introduction and scope of Geology and subdivision, Internal structure of the earth, Weathering, erosion and denudations process on earth material and natural agencies, Geological work of wind, river underground water and glaciers. Earthquakes: Basics of earthquake, earthquake history, seismic activity, concept of intensity and magnitude of earthquake, causes of earthquake, influence on civil structures and engineering consideration, seismic zonation, Stratigraphy of INDIA-Introduction.   |   8 |
|----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Study of physical properties of mineral and study of common rock forming minerals &way of formation of minerals, Study of three types of rocks with reference to their formation, identification, textural and structural features Rocks and natural materials as a construction material.Index properties of rocks, Physical and Mechanical properties, Defects in rock mass,Modes of failures of rocks.                                                                                                    |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Outcrop, stratification, dip and strike relation, Unconformity, joints their types and genesis Faults and folds with their types and causes, Engineering consideration of joints, folds and faults. Basics of Engineering Geology, Importance of geological studies to Engineers and significance of geological Investigations for civil engineering projects, Geology for Site selection of Dam, Tunnel, Reservoir and Highways.                                                                            |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Classification causes and effect of mass movements, stability of Slopes in unconsolidated materials, Influence of dip and slope Precautionary measures and control of mass movements, Case studies. Ground water and occurrence, investigations, quality, artificial recharging.                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Tunnels - Basic terminology and application, Site investigations, Construction control and maintenance, tunnel ventilation. Basics of rock mechanics in context to tunneling.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |   8 |

## HTGT501         SOIL CONTAMINATION AND GEO-ENVIRONMENTAL ENGINEERING

## Course Outcome: This course will enable students

- CO1. To explain the effects of pollutants on soil properties.
- CO2. To suggest suitable waste disposal and land treatment methods.
- CO3. To choose suitable type of land fill in accordance to waste containment principles.
- CO4. To suggest the methods for control of contamination.
- CO5. Apply soil monitoring tools and methods.

## Syllabus:

| Unit-1   | Soil and ground water pollutants - their sources, nature, composition and polluting effects. The physico-chemical aspects of soils contaminated by various pollutants. Effects of environment and wastes on the properties of soils.                                                                                                                                              |   8 |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Solid and liquid wastes disposal method and management. land treatment systems. Sources and effects of subsurface contamination; Physical, Chemical and biological characteristics of solid wastes; Soil-waste interaction; Contaminant transport; Laboratory and field evaluation of permeability; Factors affecting permeability.                                               |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Man made changes in geotechnical environment - mining, embankments, pumping, reservoir, landfills and reclamation effects and control. Waste disposal on land. Types of landfills: Sitting criteria; waste containment principles; Types of barrier materials; Planning and design aspects relating to waste disposal in landfills, in ash ponds and tailing ponds, and in rocks. |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Environmental monitoring around landfills; Detection, control and remediation of subsurface contamination; Engineering properties and geotechnical reuse of waste, demolition waste dumps; Regulations; Case studies. Control of contamination with use of clay barriers, geosynthetics, cut-off walls, proper leachate collection systems.                                       |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Regulatory norms for control of soil contamination, application of soil monitoring tools and methods Soil Pollution Controlling Methods: Different materials and techniques in control of soil pollution and treatment.                                                                                                                                                           |   8 |

## HTGT601

## FOUNDATION ENGINEERING

## Course Outcome:This course will enable students

CO1. To conductgeotechnical site investigation, sampling and field tests on soil.

CO2. To conduct soil exploration for projects.

- CO3. To evaluate the stress at depth during design of foundations.
- CO4. To design the raft foundation for buildings and tower structures.

CO5. To conclude the bearing capacity of soil from SPT and SCPT and plate load test data .

| Unit-1   | Introduction: Role of civil engineer in the selection, design and construction of foundation of civil engineering structures, brief review of soil mechanics principles used in foundation engineering. Foundations:examples of foundation problems - case studies, Characterization of ground Sampling: Source of disturbance and their influence. Type of sampler, Principle of design of sampler, Representative and undisturbed sampling in various types of soils. Surface sampling, Amount of sampling, Boring and sampling record, Preservation and shipment of sample preparation of bore log.                                                                                  |   8 |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Introduction: Necessity and Importance of soil exploration, Method of sub surface exploration Test pits, Trenches, Caissons, Tunnels and drifts, Wash boring, Percussion drilling, Rotary drilling, Factors affecting the selection of a suitable method of boring. Extent of boring, Factors controlling spacing and depth of bore holes, Spacing and depth of various Civil engineering structures.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Principles of Design of Foundations, Types of shear failures in foundation soils, Design Loads, Basic Concepts of safe and allowable bearing capacity, computation of bearing capacity in soils, effect of various factors, use of field test data in design of shallow foundations, stresses below the foundations, settlement of footings and rafts, proportioning of footings and rafts, sheeting and bracing of foundation excavation. I.S. Code method, Effect of foundation shape, eccentricity and inclination of load, Influence of soil compressibility and water table, Footing pressure for settlement on sand, Soil pressure at a depth, Boussinesq's &Westergaard methods. |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Raft foundation: Settlement and Bearing Capacity analysis, Analysis of flexible and rigid raft as per IS 2950. Computation of settlements (Immediate & Consolidation); Permissible settlements, Allowable total and differential settlement of structures.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Proportioning of footing, Inclined & Eccentric loads. Settlement of footings on stratified deposits. Influence of adjacent footings. Bearing Capacity from SPT and SCPT and Plate load Test data, proportioning of footing based on settlement criteria. Foundations on Problematic soils: Problems and Remedies.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |   8 |

- CO1. To design the various types of footings.
- CO2. To analyze and design pile foundations.
- CO3. To Examine and design various machine foundations.
- CO4. To design the earth retaining structures.
- CO5. To analyze and design the required foundation of bridges.

| Unit-1   | Building Foundation Design : Design of footing, isolated footing and steel grillage, combined footings of rectangular, Trapezoid cantilever types. Mat or raft foundation of dry and saturated soil floating foundations.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |   8 |

Well Foundations : Methods of construction tilt and shift, remedial measures, bearing capacity, settlement and lateral stability of well foundation. |   8 |

Introduction, Identification of expansive soils, Swell potential and swelling pressure, Active depth, Foundation Problems, Foundation practices in expansive soils, Soil Replacement and 'CNS' concepts.                                                                   |   8 |

Retaining walls resting on piles.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Design of Bridge Abutments, Design of Foundation for Transmission Towers : - Design of basement walls, Bridges structures Analysis and Design: Design of wells foundation and caissons of different types, Design of bridge pairs resting on piles.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |   8 |

## HTGT801 SOIL STABILIZATION AND GROUND IMPROVEMENT

## Course Outcome: This course will enable students

- CO1. To apply o ground improvement techniques in civil engineering construction activities.
- CO2. To apply geo synthetics and grouting materials for ground improvement.
- CO3. To suggest suitable ground improvement method for cohesive soil.
- CO4. To explain design step for erosion control and geo-composite drainage.
- CO5. To identify different stabilization methods and mechanism for different soils.

| Unit-1   | Introduction: Introduction to Ground Engineering Modification, Need of Ground Improvement: Different methods of Ground improvement, General Principle of Compaction: Theory of compaction, Shallow Surface Compaction - Equipment, Placement water content, factors affecting shallow compaction; Deep compaction: Methods - Vibro-floatation, Terra probe method, Pounding, Blasting, Compaction piles; Compaction Control.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |   8 |
|----------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | General survey of soil types and their behavior and the available techniques for improvement; shallow and deep mechanical modifications;modifications by admixtures and grouting,modifications by inclusions; the use of Geosynthetics material in filtration, seepage control, separation, reinforcement and water retention. Ground Improvement by Grouting and Soil Reinforcement: Grouting in soil, types of grouting material, desirable characteristics, grouting pressure, grouting methods. Soil Reinforcement: Mechanism, Types of reinforcing elements, reinforcement-soil interaction, Reinforcement of soil beneath the roads, foundation. Geosynthetics and their application. |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Ground Improvement in Cohesive Soil: Compressibility, vertical and radial consolidation, preloading methods. Types of Drains, Design of vertical Drains, construction techniques.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |   8 |
| Unit-4   | GEO Synthetics: Introduction; Geo textile, Geo jute, Geo membrane, Geo grid etc. Application of Geosynthetics in Civil Engineering, testing of geotextile, Mechanism of filtration and drainage functions & their applications, Design step for erosion control and geo-composite drainage.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Soil Stabilization: treatment of marginal soils, lime stabilization-Base exchange mechanism, Pozzolanic reaction, lime-soil interaction, line columns, Design of Foundation on lime columns. Cement stabilization: Mechanism, amount, age and curing, soil bitumen stabilization.Factors affecting stabilization of soils; Deep Mixing methods - Soil lime Columns and Cement Lime Columns, applications. Reinforced earth Principles, components of reinforced earth, governing design of reinforced earth walls, design principles of reinforced earth walls.                                                                                                                             |   8 |
