## Course Objectives:

-  To enable the students to understand about the weaving process of power loom, including different shedding mechanisms.
-  To  enable  the  students  to  understand  detailed  concepts  related  to  Dobbies  and  auxiliary motion.
-  To enable the students to understand detailed concepts related to Jacquard and its recent developments.
-  To enable the students to understand detailed concepts related to gear, pulley &amp; belt and loom shed calculations.
-  To enable the students to understand detailed concepts related to cloth calculation.

## UNIT 1

General  description  of  plain  power  looms,  introduction  topower  loom  weaving  process,  primary, secondary and auxiliary motion of plain power looms, types of sheds, tappet shedding, over and under pick motions,Beating-up Mechanism -Eccentricity of sley, Timing and Synchronization of Primary motion,Temple and its utility, Negative and positive take up motion, five wheel and  seven wheel take up motion, positive &amp;negativelet-off motions.

Contact hours required = 9

## UNIT 2

Scope &amp; limitation of dobby, negative and positive dobby,  right hand &amp; left hand dobby &amp; their pegging methods, Development in dobby, brief description of Cromption and Knowles dobby, cross border dobby, heald  reversing  motion. Warp protective mechanism- loose reed &amp; fast reed, weft detection mechanismsside and center weft fork motion.

Contact hours required = 9

## UNIT 3

Types of jacquards and their principle of working, size and figuring capacity of jacquard, Single lift single cylinder  Jacquard,  Double  lift  single  cylinder,  Double  lift  double  cylinder,  split  harness,  cross  border jacquards, Different system of harness tie- up, terry mechanism, Recent developments in jacquard weaving.

Contact hours required = 9

## UNIT 4

Multiple  box  motion  and  their  types,  Pick-at will,  pick  and  pick  motion.  Automatic  loom:  pirn  change, shuttle  change  loom,  detailed  study  of  various  motions  of  automatic  looms,  mechanical  warp  stop motions, electro-mechanical warp stop motion.

Contact hours required = 9

## UNIT 5

Gear, pully &amp; belt Calculations. Production and efficiency of machine    e, take up and let off, Jacquard Design and Harness Calculations - Calculations related to Sett of harness, sett of warp, ends per repeat, size of repeat, number of repeats, symmetrical repeat setting for straight draft and pointed draft, number of cords per hook and casting out. Loom shed calculation - Efficiency, Production and Production Planning.

Contact hours required = 8

## OUTCOMES:

Upon completion of the course the student will be able to:-

-  Understand the working principle of power looms, dobby and jacquard.
-  Understand the calculation related to machines, production and efficiency.
