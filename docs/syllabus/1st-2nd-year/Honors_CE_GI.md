## Syllabus

## HTGI 401                     REMOTE SENSING &amp; PHOTOGRAMMETRY

Course Outcomes: After completion of this course the student will be able to:

- CO1. Understand basics of remote sensing and related concepts
- CO2. Understand  evolutionof  satellite  technology, ISRO's  role, and  significanceof global  satellite programs.
- CO3. Understand microwave remote sensing and related concepts.
- CO4. Understand concepts of photogrammetry, including DPWS, input selection, scanner principles, and interpretation for mapping.
- CO5. Understand LiDAR principles, error sources and ALTM for accurate terrain mapping.

## Syllabus:

| Unit-1   | REMOTE SENSING: Introduction to remote sensing and related concepts, Optical remote sensing,EMR interaction with earth and environment, satellites and sensors, spatial/spectral/radiometric and temporal resolutions, Characteristics of images, Passive and active sensing, Multispectral and Hyperspectral scanning, Thermal remote sensing, Infra-Red detection and imaging technology, thermal sensors and scanners, airborne IR surveys, satellite thermal IR images, interpretation of thermal IR imagery, Computation of Land Surface Temperature.   |   8 |
|----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | INDIAN & INTERNATIONAL SATELLITE PROGRAMS: Overview of satellite technology, Early satellites and space exploration milestones, Establishment of ISRO, Indian satellite launch vehicles, Notable Indian satellite missions, ISRO's contributions to international space community. Important Satellite Programs such as Landsat program, SPOT, Sentinel, MODIS, Carto sat, SARAL, Resource sat, Various geo portals for accessing satellite data.                                                                                                            |   8 |
| Unit-3   | MICROWAVE REMOTE SENSING Introduction to microwave remote sensing, Overview of non-imaging and imaging microwave sensors, principles, physical fundamentals, Scattering of Microwaves, Interaction of Microwaves with the Atmosphere; Interaction with discrete objects, Fundamentals of Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR), Basics of Image formation, Radar equation, Image defects - Geometric distortions, Introduction to Sentinel Application Platform (SNAP).                                                                                             |   8 |
| Unit-4   | PHOTOGRAMMETRY: Elements of Photographic systems,Three Phases of Photogrammetry (Analogue, Analytical and Digital), Advantages of Digital Photogrammetry, Various Inputs for Digital Photogrammetry: Control Point selection, Scanned Photo, Digital Photographs. Remote Sensing Imagery Photogrammetric Scanners: Principle of Image Scanning, Configuration of Scanners, Method of Scanning, File Format and Size. Introduction & Digital Photogrammetric Work Stations (DPWS),Hardware & Software Components of DPWS.                                     |   8 |
| Unit-5   | AIR-BORNE LASER TERRAIN MAPPING (ALTM): Active remote sensing technology, Introduction to Laser Ranging, Principle of LiDAR, System Components, Range Measurements, LiDAR Error Sources, Accuracy, Applications &Advantages of ALTM.Drone mapping.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |   8 |

## HTGI 501         MACHINE PROCESSING OF REMOTELY SENSED DATA

## Course Outcomes: After completion of this course the student will be able to:

- CO1. Understand satellite  data  acquisition  systems,  data  storage,  formats,  and  compression  techniques, along with an overview of current remote sensing systems.
- CO2. Describe  the  preprocessing  of  remotely  sensed  data,  correcting  radiometric  and  geometric distortions, and gaining insight into spectral analysis.
- CO3. Apply skills in image enhancement and analysis, including contrast manipulation, convolution for edge enhancement, and spatial feature manipulation.
- CO4. Explore image transformations, pattern recognition, and classification techniques in remote sensing, along with image fusion and change detection methods.
- CO5. Explain  GIS  integration,  multitemporal  data  merging,  hyper-spectral  image  analysis,  and  radar image analysis techniques in the context of remote sensing applications.

## Syllabus:

| Unit-1   | SATELLITE DATA ACQUISITION AND IMAGE PROCESSING BASICS: Overview of satellite data acquisition systems, Initial Statics of Satellite data, Storage and retrieval of remotely sensed data, Understanding data formats and compression techniques, Introduction to satellite systems and their data products, Image display systems and an overview of current remote sensing systems.   |   8 |
|----------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | PREPROCESSING OF REMOTELY SENSED DATA: Radiometric and geometric distortions in remotely sensed data, Techniques and methods for geometric correction, Radiometric correction and noise removal, Spectral ratioing and introduction to principal and canonical components, Vegetative components in remote sensing data.                                                               |   8 |
| Unit-3   | IMAGE ENHANCEMENT AND ANALYSIS: Image rectification and restoration techniques, Contrast manipulation and gray-level thresholding, Level slicing and contrast stretching, Convolution for edge enhancement, Spatial feature manipulation in image processing.                                                                                                                          |   8 |
| Unit-4   | IMAGE TRANSFORMATIONS AND PATTERN RECOGNITION: Overview of image transformations in remote sensing, Pattern recognition techniques in image analysis, Shape analysis, textural, and contextual analysis, Image classification Techniques, Image fusion and change detection in remote sensing data.                                                                                    |   8 |
| Unit-5   | GIS INTEGRATION, HYPER-SPECTRAL, AND RADAR IMAGE ANALYSIS: Data merging and integration with GIS , Multitemporal data merging and change detection procedures , Multisensor image merging and incorporating ancillary data , Introduction to hyper-spectral image analysis and atmospheric correction , Radar image analysis techniques.                                               |   8 |

## HTGI 601             GLOBAL NAVIGATION SATELLITE SYSTEM

## Course Outcomes: After completion of this course the student will be able to:

- CO1. Understand  celestial  coordinate  systems,  observational  astronomy,  telescopic  surveying,  and  the role of astrometry in precise celestial measurements.
- CO2. Describe  GPS  fundamentals,  including  components,  signal  structure,  navigation  principles, accuracy assessment.
- CO3. Analyze GPS applications in astronomy and astrophysics through case studies.
- CO4. Illustrate  the  principles  of  Differential  GPS  (DGPS),  including  differential  corrections,  various DGPS methods.
- CO5. Apply GPS and DGPS knowledge to military and civilian navigation, surveying, mapping, remote sensing.

## Syllabus:

| Unit-1   | INTRODUCTION TO ASTRONOMICAL SURVEYING: Overview of astronomical surveying and its applications in astronomy and astrophysics, Celestial coordinate systems and their significance in surveying, Basics of observational astronomy and the use of telescopes in surveying, Introduction to astrometry and its role in precise celestial measurements.                                                                                         |   8 |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | GPS FUNDAMENTALS: Introduction to Global Positioning System (GPS) and its components, GPS signal structure and satellite constellation, Principles of satellite-based navigation and positioning, GPS accuracy, error sources, and correction methods, Factors that affect GPS,Indian satellite based navigational systems: Navigation with Indian Constellation (NavIC), GAGAN,Other satellite based navigational systems: GLONASS, GALILEO. |   8 |
| Unit-3   | ASTRONOMICAL COORDINATE SYSTEMS AND GPS APPLICATIONS: Advanced celestial coordinate systems in astronomical surveying, Integration of GPS technology in astronomical observations, Astrodynamics and satellite orbits for precise GPS applications, Case studies of GPS applications in astronomy and astrophysics.                                                                                                                           |   8 |
| Unit-4   | DGPS: History, need for DGPS, concepts and principles, differential corrections, accuracy in DGPS, local area DGPS, wide area DGPS, carrier phase DGPS, pseudolites, LAAS, WAAS; rapid methods with GPS - rapid static method, semikinematic method, kinematic method. Real time DGPS. Data post-processing; GIS and GPS integration; Georeferencing of map.                                                                                  |   8 |
| Unit-5   | APPLICATIONS OF GPS AND DGPS: Military - airborne, marine and land based navigation, and civilian -surveying and mapping, control surveys, cadastral surveying, navigation, RS, GIS and photogrammetry, geodesy, location, navigation, tracking, mapping and timing, Engineering and Monitoring; Special applications of GPS, Applications of high-precision GPS in astronomical surveying.                                                   |   8 |

## HTGI 701             GEOGRAPHICAL INFORMATION SYSTEM

## Course Outcomes: After completion of this course the student will be able to:

- CO1. Explore the integration of GIS with Geospatial AI for enhanced spatial analysis.
- CO2. Apply GIS and GPS technology for spatial data acquisition, effective management of data for the projects.
- CO3. Apply skills in cartography, spatial analysis and geospatial modelling.
- CO4. Integrate machine learning models with GIS data, with a focus on Artificial Neural Network (ANN) analysis.
- CO5. Apply deep learning techniques to data acquired from Remote Sensing, GPS, and RFID.

## HTGI 801               APPLICATIONS OF GEOINFORMATICS

## Course Outcomes: After completion of this course the student will be able to:

- CO1. Illustrate Digital Elevation Models (DEMs), encompassing raster, vector, and point cloud data.
- CO2. Explain  DEM  acquisition  techniques,  including  photogrammetry,  satellite  interferometry,  and LiDAR, along with preprocessing steps like filtering, resampling, and mosaicking.
- CO3. Apply DEM technology to forest and water applications.
- CO4. Explore urban applications of DEMs, focusing on urban and regional planning, land use planning, urban growth monitoring, transportation system studies, and slum detection.
- CO5. Understand the role of DEMs in disaster management, with a focus on GIS applications in risk assessment, mitigation, and preparedness for disasters.

## Syllabus:

| Unit-1   | DIGITAL ELEVATION MODELS (DEMS): Raster, Vector, and Point Cloud Data, DEM Definitions, Additional Related Definitions: Mass Points, Breaklines, and Spot Elevations. Triangulated Irregular Networks (TINs), Esri Terrain Datasets and LAS Datasets, Contours. 3-D Surface Modeling, Topographic Surface Modeling, Hydrologic Surface Modeling., Topographic and Hydrologic Surface Examples., Elevation Derivatives, Creating Raster Surfaces (Interpolation Methods), Terrain Visualization, Virtual Reality Data sources for DEM creation: satellite data, LiDAR, stereo imagery.                                            |   8 |
|----------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | DEM ACQUISITION AND PROCESSING: Techniques for DEM acquisition: photogrammetry, satellite interferometry, LiDAR. Preprocessing steps for DEM creation: filtering, resampling, and mosaicking. Accuracy assessment and quality control in DEMgeneration.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |   8 |
| Unit-3   | FOREST & WATER APPLICATIONS: Forests - Forest type classification using multispectral data and density mapping. Forest stock mapping. Forest change detection. Forest fire detection and burned area mapping and fire vulnerability assessment. Applications of Laser in vegetation studies - Aerial and terrestrial. Water -Surface water resources assessment and management, Reservoir sedimentation. Performance evaluation of command areas. Integrated watershed development, water quality monitoring and mapping. Wetland mapping. Snow and Glaciers: Snow Cover Mapping, Glacier Mapping, Forecasting snow melt runoff. |   8 |
| Unit-4   | URBAN APPLICATIONS: Concepts of Urban and Regional planning and its applications, urban services and network planning, Urban landuse planning Urban growth /Sprawl; Slum detection, monitoring and updating, Study of Transportation Systems.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |   8 |
| Unit-5   | DISASTER MANAGEMENT: Types of disasters - earthquakes and Tsunamis - volcanoes - landslides. Use of GIS in Risk Assessment, Mitigation & Preparedness.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |   8 |
