## HTFT401                     SIMULATION AND MODELING IN TEXTILES

## Course Objective:

- To understand the basic concept of simulation and modeling
- To learn the modeling of yarn manufacturing processes
- To study the modeling of fabric manufacturing processes
- To understand the modeling of fabric property

## Unit 1

Introduction of simulation and modeling techniques and its usefulness in textile processes.

## Unit 2

Different  simulation  techniques,  Monte  Carlo  simulation  with  applications;  mathematical modeling,  concept  of  fuzzy  model,  Mamdani  and  TSK  inference  system;  artificial  neural network,  training  algorithms;  Supervised,  Unsupervised  and  Reinforced  learning;  expert system, Combination of different systems, comparison of different techniques.

## Unit 3

Simulation of fibre properties,  carding  process, drafting,  yarn  formation,  package  building, balloon theory.

## Unit 4

Modeling of weaving and knitting process, monitoring of online process performance.

## Unit 5

Modelling of fabric low stress properties.

## HTFT501                                                CLOTHING COMFORT

## Course Outcomes

- To study various factors involved in clothing comfort
- To learn about heat relation in clothing
- To study about moisture relation in clothing
- To study about physical properties of clothing and clothing materials in relation to comfort
- To learn current trends and new developments in clothing science

## Unit 1

Factors involved in the study of clothing: the balance needed for comfort; boarder aspects of  comfort;  nonthermal  components  of  comfort;  environmental  ranges  and  corresponding clothing.

## Unit 2

Heat relation in clothing : approaches for studying heat exchange in clothing systems; heat factors in clothing as worn; heat transmission properties of clothing materials.

## Unit 3

Moisture  relation  in  clothing :  approaches  for  studying  moisture  exchange  in  clothing systems; moisture factors in clothing as worn; moisture transmission properties of clothing materials.

## Unit 4

Physical properties of clothing and clothing  materials in relation to comfort : thermal transmission  or  resistance;  water  vapor  diffusion  resistance;  water  transport  in  clothing materials;  water  holding  properties;  thickness  of  clothing  materials;  surface  properties  of fabrics; porosity and permeability properties; flammability of clothing materials; stiffness and bending properties etc.

## Unit 5

Current  trends  and  new  developments  in  the  study  of  clothing: new  materials  and finishes; new techniques and new concepts.

## References

- Lyman Fourt and Norman R.S. Hollies, 'Clothing Comfort and Function', Marcel Dekker, INC., New York, 1971
- Kothari, V K, 'Testing and Quality Management ', CBS Book Publishers, New Delhi, 2000.
- Zhang Wei Yuan , ' Clothing Comfort and Function',China Textile Press,2011
- Saville B P, 'Physical Testing of Textiles,' The Textile Institute, Woodhead Publication Limited, Cambridge, 1999.

## HTFT601                              SUSTAINABLE TEXTILE TECHNOLOGIES

## Course Outcomes

- To understand the importance of sustainable solutions for protecting the environment
- To utilize the energy efficient processes for optimally utilization of the scarce resources
- To analyze the need of the society for recycling and reusing of the textile materials
- To acquire the knowledge about effluent treatment techniques (ZDHC) for protecting the water sources

## Unit 1

Introduction: Present scenario in various sectors of textile industries and its consequences on growth of industry. Sustainability and its significance in the pretext of textile industries Approaches to different forms of sustainable procedures, logistics systems, value chains and Green technology in textiles

## Unit 2

Energy efficient processes: Various techniques to reduce wastage of energy. Ascertaining boiler  capacity  with  production  volume,  Precautions  to  reduce  wastage  of  steam  and electrical energy.

## Unit 3

Modified  technologies  for  chemical  processes: Modified  technologies  of colouration, Processing  with  low  liquor  ratio,  Mass  colouration  of  textiles,  Continuous  application techniques,  Dyeing  of  blends,  Bioprocessing  of  textiles,  Controlled  application  techniques, Combination of various processes like, desizing - scouring, scouring - bleaching etc.,Dyeing at room temperature, viz. vat, reactive and direct dyeing of cotton.

## Unit 4

Waste  reduction  techniques :  Need  for  solid  and  hazardous  waste  management  in  textile industry,  types  and  sources  of  solid  and  hazardous  waste  management  in  textile  industry, Waste  water  management,  Norms  of  using  water  in  processing  and  discharge  to  public sewage. Various technical approaches to reduce waste water load. Analysis of effluent, Zero Discharge of Hazardous Chemicals (ZDHC)

## Unit 5

Recycling of textiles and its waste: Concepts of Linear and Circular economy; Life Cycle Analysis, Cradle  to  gate  and  Cradle  to  grave;  Goal  and  Scope,  inventory  analysis,  Impact assessment; Life cycle of textile products, recycling of polymer and fibre waste, recycling of waste fibres and fabrics for nonwoven fabric production; Recycled materials standard.

## HTFT801                APPLICATION OF OPERATIONS RESEARCH IN TEXTILES

## Course Outcomes

- To study the concept of research and managerial decision making
- To learn optimization technique, primarily LPP
- To study application of operations research in textile operations
- To learn transportation and assignment problems and the optimization techniques
- To study the concept of inventory management

## Unit 1

Operation research - Introduction, historical development, phases of operation research study; Linear  programming;  graphical  method;  simplex  method;  Sensitivity  analysis;  Duality; Introduction to goal programming; Application of LPP in yarn production

## Unit 2

Transportation problem and its application in textile production; Methods of finding an initial solution  (North-West  Corner;  Vogel's  approximation),  Methods  to  get  the  final  solution (MODI and stepping stone), degeneracy, optimum solution

## Unit 3

Assignment problems, variation in assignment problems, queuing theory

## Unit 4

Inventory  management  techniques-  selective  inventory  control:  ABC  analysis,  economic order  quantity  (EOQ);  EOQ  models  (instantaneous  receipt  and  non-instantaneous  receipt); EOQ  models  with  planned  shortage;  Inventory  policies  (Periodic  review  and  Continuous review policies), ordering cost, acquisition cost, inventory carrying cost or holding cost, just in time

## Unit 5

Information systems for inventory management, store management and merchandising, make or buy decision, analysis of investment in inventory, value analysis and material management; Enterprise resource planning.
