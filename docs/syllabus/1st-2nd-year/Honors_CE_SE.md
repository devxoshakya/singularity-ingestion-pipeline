## Syllabus

## HTSE401                      CONSTRUCTION ENGINEERING

## Course Outcomes: After completion of this course the student will be able to

CO1. To understand the need of services in different types of buildings.

- CO2. To understand Air Conditioning &amp; Ventilation.
- CO3. To understand the Acoustics of the buildings.
- CO4. To understand the Thermal Insulation and Building Byelaws.
- CO5. To understand the physical, social and economic parameters for regional planning.

## Syllabus:

| Unit-1   | Services in Residential and Commercial Buildings: Sanitation, Water supply, Electrical wiring, Rain water disposal, Lighting and illumination, Calculation for these services.                                                                                                                                                                                                    |   6 |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Air Conditioning & Ventilation: Natural ventilation, Control cooling systems, Modern systems of air conditioning, Ducting systems, Different mechanical means of air conditioning.                                                                                                                                                                                                |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Acoustics: General Principles of transmission and passage of sound, Reverberation, Absorption, Reflection, Acoustic materials and their co- efficiency, Principles of good acoustic design.                                                                                                                                                                                       |   6 |
| Unit-4   | Thermal Insulation: Behaviour of various building materials & thermal conductivity, Fire safety devices, Thermal insulation for air conditioned interior spaces, Working out air conditioning loads for different spaces. Architectural Control &Building Bylaws: Role of buildings byelaws in a city, Local byelaws and architectural controls, Façade control and zoning plans. |  10 |
| Unit-5   | Regional Planning: Understanding of physical, social and economic parameters for regional planning. Landscaping: Forces of man and nature, their relationship and effect on shaping the landscape, Site analysis, Site and structure relationship and landscape assessment.                                                                                                       |  10 |

## HTSE501                        REHABILITATION OF STRUCTURES

## Course Outcomes: After completion of this course the student will be able to

CO1. Explain the need for rehabilitation.

- CO2. Asses the cause, effects and Damage of structures.
- CO3. Apply the Damage testing methods.
- CO4. Apply methods of rehabilitation in structures.
- CO5. Apply the concepts of structural health monitoring.

## Syllabus:

| Unit-3   | Damage Testing Methods: Non Destructive Tests and Core cutting methods.                                                                                             |   6 |
| Unit-4   | Rehabilitation Methods: Repair and rehabilitation of buildings, Seismic strengthening of structures, use of carbon plates, FRP etc. for retrofitting of structures. |  10 |
| Unit-5   | Concepts of structural health monitoring.                                                                                                                           |  10 |

## HTSE601                              ADVANCED STRUCTURAL ANALYSIS

## Course Outcomes: After completion of this course the student will be able to

CO1. Analyse the structures using Stiffness Matrix Method.

CO2. Analyse the structures using Flexibility Matrix Method.

CO3. Apply the concepts of Finite element method in structures.

## Syllabus:

| Unit-1   | Stiffness Matrix Method: Basis of stiffness method, Influence coefficients, Kinematic indeterminacy, Degree of freedom, Action displacement relationship, Matrix approach to stiffness method, Transformation of axes system, Formation of load vectors, Elastic supports, Support displacements,   |   6 |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Application of stiffness matrix method to various type of structures e.g. Continuous beams, Trusses, Frames and grids, partially discontinuous structures, Temperature effects.                                                                                                                     |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Flexibility Matrix Method: Compatibility equations, Flexibility coefficients, Application of complimentary energy principles, Basis of the method,                                                                                                                                                  |   6 |
| Unit-4   | Application of flexibility matrix method to various types of structures, Analysis of pin jointed trusses, Rigid frames.                                                                                                                                                                             |  10 |
| Unit-5   | Finite Element Method: Introduction to finite element method, Theory of elasticity, Coordinate systems, Rotation of axes, Shape functions, Elements stiffness matrix and load Vector. Triangular element in plane stress and strain, Numerical integration,                                         |  10 |

## Text Books:

1. Matrix Analysis of Framed Structures by Gere and Weaver.
2. Analysis of Indeterminate Structures by C.K. Wang.
3. Finite Element Methods by Zeiekiwitz and Cheung.
4. Advance Structural Analysis by A.K.Jain.
5. Introduction to Finite Element Method by C.S.Desai and John F. Abel.

## HTSE701            ADVANCED REINFORCED CONCRETE STRUCTURES

## Course Outcomes: After completion of this course the student will be able to

- CO1. Design the flat slabs as per code of practice.
- CO2. Design the deep beams.
- CO3. Design the over head service reservoir.
- CO4. Design various slabs using Yield Line analysis.
- CO5. Design the Beam Column Joints.

| Unit-1   | Flat Slabs: Elements of flat slabs, Codal procedure for design of flat slabs, Behaviour of flat slab in shear, One way and two way shear, Equivalent Frame Method, Openings in flat slabs, Effect of pattern loading in flat slabs.                      |   8 |
|----------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Deep Beams: General features, Parameter influencing design, Flexural bending and shear stresses in deep beams. Design provisions of IS-456, Checking for local failures, Strut and tie analysis of deep beams, Detailing of reinforcement in deep beams. |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Over Head Service Reservoir: Special design considerations, Design requirements of materials, membrane analysis and compatibility analysis of reservoir, complete design and drawing details of an overhead service reservoir.                           |  10 |
| Unit-4   | Yield Line Analysis: Design of slabs of various shapes and having various support conditions using yield line analysis approach.                                                                                                                         |   6 |
| Unit-5   | Design of Beam Column Joints: Types of joints, Joints inmultistoried buildings, Forces acting on joints, Design of joints for strength, Anchorage requirement in joints and detailing of reinforcement in joints.                                        |   8 |

## Text Books:

1. Advanced R.C. Design by Krishna Raju.
2. Limit State Design by A.K. Jain.
3. Reinforced Concrete Structures by Park and Pauley.
4. Reinforced Concrete Structural Elements - Behaviour Analysis and Design by Purushothaman.
5. Concrete Technology by M.S. Shetty.

## HTSE801                         PRE-STRESSED CONCRETE STRUCTURES

## Course Outcomes: After completion of this course the student will be able to

- CO1. Explain the systems of pre-stressing.
- CO2. Evaluate stresses in pre stressing cables.
- CO3. Explain stress distribution in end blocks.
- CO4. Design various structural elements.
- CO5. Design the composite beams and concrete pipes.

## Syllabus:

| Unit-1   | Principles of pre-stressing, materials of pre-stressing, pre-stressing systems, losses of prestress, short term and long term deflections of PSC members.   |   8 |
|----------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Cable profile and cable layout, concept of load balancing and stresses in cables.                                                                           |   6 |
| Unit-3   | End blocks, Stress distribution in end blocks, Anchorage zone reinforcement.                                                                                |   6 |
| Unit-4   | Design of PSC continuous beams, slab and domes, Analysis and design of PSC members for flexure, shear, bond, torsion and bearing.                           |  10 |
| Unit-5   | Concepts of design of composite beams Circular pre-stressing, Types of PSC pipes, Design of PSC concrete pipes.                                             |  10 |

## Text Books:

1. Design of Prestressed Concrete structures by T.Y. Lin .
2. Prestressed Concrete by G. Magnel.
3. Fundamentals of Prestressed Concrete by V. Natarajan.
4. Prestressed Concrete by Krishna Raju.
