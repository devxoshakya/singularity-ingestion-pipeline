## Syllabus

## HTEN401                                EARTH AND ENVIRONMENT

## Course Outcomes: At the end of this course students will demonstrate the ability to:

- CO1. Explain the function of an ecosystem with reference to nutrient dynamics and energy flow.
- CO2. Explain  the  ways  that  organisms  interact  with  both  the  physical  and  the  biological environment (biotic and abiotic).
- CO3. Demonstrate  the  biogeochemical  cycles  and  their  significance  in  the  sustainability  of ecosystems.
- CO4. Attempt to resolve the major energy issues and challenges of the 21st century.
- CO5. Suggest the suitable renewable energy resources and their major role in dealing with the recent energy crisis of the world.

| Unit-1   | Introduction: Scope and Importance of Clean Environment, Ecosystem, Balanced Ecosystem, Components of Ecosystem, Biosphere, Biodiversity, Assimilation Capacity of Environment.                              |   8 |
|----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Unit-2   | Environmental pollution: Causes and Remedial Measures: Air, Water, Noise, Thermal Pollution, Radioactive and Non-Radioactive Pollution, Soil and Land Pollution.                                             |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Impact of Human Activities on Environment: Mining, Deforestation, Agriculture, Industry, Transportation, Urbanization and Development.                                                                       |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Current environmental issues: Climate Change, Population Growth, Green House Effect and Global Warming, Depletion of Ozone, e-Waste, Plastic Waste, Automobile Pollution and Acid Rain.                      |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Conservation of environment: Approaches to conserve environment: Conventional and Non-Conventional Energy Resources, Need of public awareness, Sustainable Development and Goals of Sustainable Development. |   8 |

## HTEN501                      INDUSTRIAL WASTE WATER TREATMENT

## Course Outcomes: At the end of this course students will demonstrate the ability to:

- CO1. Explain the industrial growth of India during various plans since independence.
- CO2. Choose appropriate methods for repairing and rebuilding process of industrial growth.
- CO3. Explain the trends of industrial growth.
- CO4. Describe the ways to dispose the waste and how to recycle the generated waste in the proper way.
- CO5. Identify the standards set by Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB), India for the discharge of waste in water bodies.

## HTEN601                                            SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT

## Course Outcomes: At the end of this course students will demonstrate the ability to:

- CO1. Apply methods of resource management and solid waste management.
- CO2. Choose suitable solid waste management infrastructure systems.
- CO3. Discuss  the  strategies,  resource  management,  recycling,  reuse  and  reclamation  of  solid wastes.
- CO4. Identify the inappropriate waste management practices and their impacts on water, soil and sediment quality.
- CO5. Compare  the  available  practices  and  systems    for  sustainable  resource  and  solid  waste management.

## HTEN701                   AIR AND NOISE POLLUTION AND CONTROL

## Course Outcomes: At the end of this course students will demonstrate the ability to:

- CO1. Define air pollution and its effects.
- CO2. Plan measurement and monitoring of air pollutants.
- CO3. Prepare emission inventories and evaluates results.
- CO4. Question effects of meteorology on air pollution.
- CO5. Discuss air pollution modeling.

| Unit-4   | Control of gaseous pollutants: Process and equipment for the removal by chemical methods - Design and operation of absorption and adsorption equipment - Combustion and condensation equipment, fugitive gas emissions and control. Automobile Pollution And Control: Sources, Theoretical Considerations, Operating conditions Vs Emissions, Pollution control Measures, Emission Standards Industrial Pollution, Vehicular Pollution, Indoor Air Pollution. |   8 |

## HTEN801                            ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT ASSESSMENT

## Course Outcomes: At the end of this course students will demonstrate the ability to:

- CO1. Explain the major principles of environmental impact assessment in India.
- CO2. Understand the different steps within environmental impact assessment.
- CO3. Discuss the implications of current jurisdictional and institutional arrangements in relation to environmental impact assessment.
- CO4. Communicate both orally and in written form the key aspects of environmental impact assessment.
- CO5. Understand how to liaise with and the importance of stakeholders in the EIA process.

| Unit-2   | EIA methodologies: Introduction, Criteria for the selection of EIA Methodology, EIA methods, Ad-hoc methods, matrix methods, Network method Environmental Media Quality Index method, overlay methods, cost/Benefit Analysis.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |   8 |
| Unit-3   | Impact of developmental activities and land use: Introduction, Methodology for the assessment of soil and ground water, Delineation of study area, Identification of activities. Assessment of Impact of development Activities on Vegetation and wildlife, environmental Impact of Deforestation - Causes and effects of deforestation.                                                                                                                                                             |   8 |
| Unit-4   | Prediction and assessment of impact: Quality, Impact prediction, Assessment of Impact significance, Identification, and Incorporation of mitigation measures. EIA in surface water, Air and Biological environment: Methodology for the assessment of Impacts on surface water environment, Air pollution sources, generalized approach for assessment of Air pollution Impact.                                                                                                                      |   8 |
| Unit-5   | Environmental audit & environmental legislation: objectives of Environmental Audit, Types of environmental Audit, Audit protocol, stages of Environmental Audit, on-site activities, evaluation of Audit data and preparation of Audit report. Post Audit activities: The Environmental pollution Act, The water; Act, the Air (Prevention & Control of pollution Act.), MOTA Act. Wild life Act. Case studies and preparation: of Environmental Impact assessment statement for various Industries. |   8 |
