## MNTT401                                                   MEDICAL TEXTILES

## Unit 1

Historical  development  of  medical  garments,  Classification  of  medical  textiles,  Protective function  domain,  Treatment  function  domain,  caring  function  domain,  Development  of medical garments,   Trends of medical garments, Future scope of medical garments

## Unit 2

Review  of  medical  garments  for  protective  function,  Medical  drapes  and  gowns,  Textile materials  for  medical  drapes  and  gowns,  Design  considerations  for  medical  gowns,  Hip protectors,  Textiles  used  for  hip  protector,  Design  considerations  for  improvement  on  the compliance,  Medical garments for treatment function

## Unit 3

Pressure  garments  (PGs),  Textiles  used  for  PG,    Considerations  on  the  design  of  PGs, Compression  stockings  (CSs),    Considerations  on  the  application  of  CSs,    Wet  dressings, Functions of wet dressings,  Textiles used for wet dressings, Limitations of wet dressings

## Unit 4

Features of apparel for caring function, Superabsorbent materials (SAMs) used in disposable healthcare  products,  Wound  dressings,    Diapers  for  incontinence  problems,  Intelligent medical  garments,    Application  of  conductive  textile  material  into  the  smart  garment, Examples  of  intelligent  medical  garments,  Smart  garments  for  vital  signs  and  motion monitoring, Smart diapers

## Unit 5

Case studies in the development of apparel for the elderly and the disabled, Stoma bag, Under plants, Urine bag pants -- 62. 1. Problems faced by people currying a urine bag, Techniques adopted for the development of urine bag pants, Sun block protective garments,

## References

- Medical Textile Materials, Y Qin, Woodhead Publishing, 21-Nov-2015
- Advances in Smart Medical  Textiles: Treatments and Health Monitoring, L V Langenhove, Woodhead Publishing, 02-Dec-2015
- Textiles for Hygiene and Infection Control, B J McCarthy, Elsevier Publication, 2011
- Antimicrobial Textiles, G Sun, Woodhead Publishing, 11-Apr-2016

## Course Objective:

- To learn the basic concept of automotive textiles
- To learn the manufacturing processes of automotive textiles
- To learn the features of required raw materials for automotive textiles
- To  study  the  characterization  techniques  and  their  data  interpretation  to  evaluate  the automotive textiles
- To understand the country's position in this field and understand the planning to become manufacturing hub of automotive textiles

## Unit 1 Automotive Textiles

A New Technology for High Performance Inflatable SUPs, Elastic behavior of composites containing  multi-layer  coated  particles  with  imperfect  interface  bonding  conditions  and application to size effects and mismatch in these composites

Interior  design;  Fabric  structures  and  production  methods;  Yarn  and  fabric  processing; Quality assurance and testing; Product engineering -interior trim; Other textile applications; Automotive  textiles and  the environment;  Textiles  in other forms  of transportation; Future development and outlook.

## Unit 2

Air bags: Importance of Airbag in Automobiles, Airbag Fabrics, Treatment of airbag fabrics, Airjet  textured  yarns  for  Airbag  fabrics,  Nonlinear  behaviour  of  airbag  fabrics,  Material selection  for  Airbag  fabrics,  Manufacturing  of  Airbag  fabrics,  Effect  of  Heat  on  the Properties of Automotive Airbag Materials

## Unit 3

Seat Belts: Importance, seat belt webbing, seat belt standards,  design and fabrication of automobile seat belt system, Indian standards for Safety belts, Fibres used in seat belt webbing, weaving of seat belt webbing

## Unit 4

Fabric filters: Introduction, Types of Fabric Filters, Shaker Cleaning, Reverse-air Cleaning, Pulse-jet Cleaning, Caged Filters, Cartridge Filters, Sonic Cleaning, Fabric Filtration Theory,

Reverse  Air/Shake  Deflate  Baghouses,  Pulse-Jet  Baghouses,  Design  Procedure,  Pressure Drop, Particle Characteristics, Effect of temperature and pressure on filtration efficiency

## Unit 5

Seat  cover  fabrics:  woven  seat  cover  fabrics,  weft  knitted  fabrics,  Spacer  Fabrics,  Velvet fabrics,  Flock  fabrics,    Testing  of  seat  cover  fabrics,  Interlining  materials  for  seat  cover fabrics, Moisture transport through car seat cover fabrics, resiliency, Comfort and safety in car seat cover fabrics

## Protective Textiles

## Unit 1

Overview  of  protective  clothing,  Standards  for  protective  textiles,  Fashion  and  function  factors affecting the design and use of protective clothing, Steps in the selection of protective clothing materials

## Unit 2

Fibres and fabrics for protective textiles, Technical textiles for protection, Intelligent textiles for protection, Surface treatments for protective textiles

## Unit 3

Evaluation  of  protective  clothing  systems  using  manikins,  Interactions  between  protection and  thermal  comfort,  Modeling  thermal  burn  injury  protection,  Civilian  protection  and protection of industrial workers from chemicals

## Unit 4

Fundamentals of cold weather clothing comfort: Achieving comfort and thermoregulation in  cold  weather  clothing.  Materials  for  cold  weather  clothing:  Coated  and  laminated fabrics  for  cold  weather  apparel;  Wool  for  cold  weather  clothing,  Design  considerations and  ergonomics:  Designing  for  ventilation;  Adaptive  systems  for  cold  weather  clothing. Evaluation of cold weather apparel: Laboratory assessment of cold weather clothing Extreme cold and heat protective textiles, Thermal (heat and fire) protection, Microorganism protection,  Textiles  for  respiratory  protection,  Electrostatic  protection,  Ballistic  protection, Chemical and biological protection
